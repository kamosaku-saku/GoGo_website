About USTEP

What is USTEP?

USTEP stands for University-wide Student Exchange Program. It is the University of Tokyo’s official student exchange program.

A student exchange program is a system in which the University of Tokyo and its partner universities exchange students for a period of one semester to one year.

One of the key advantages of USTEP is that UTokyo students continue to pay tuition to the University of Tokyo, meaning they are not required to pay tuition fees at the host university.

Each year, approximately 300 students participate in USTEP, studying abroad at around 90 partner universities worldwide.

It is important to note that applications must be submitted around one year before departure, so careful and early preparation is essential.

Application cycles

  • Autumn application: for study abroad starting in the following autumn semester or the spring semester of the year after.
  • Spring application: for study abroad starting in the following spring semester.

For more details, please visit the official Go Global website .

How to Apply

The application process for USTEP is primarily based on document screening.

Applications are submitted through UTAS, an online system used by University of Tokyo students and faculty to manage academic procedures.

Application requirements

  • Basic information, such as name and contact details
  • Academic record, including GPA coefficient calculated on a 3.0 scale
  • A recommendation letter from a professor
  • Preferred partner universities, up to 6 choices
  • Several written essays

Written essays

  • Extracurricular activities, personal strengths, and experiences, approximately 1000 words
  • Motivation for studying abroad, approximately 2000 words
  • Study and research plan at the host university, approximately 6000 words
  • Future plans and career goals, approximately 2000 words

These sections require a significant amount of time, so it is important to start preparing early.

What to Do After Acceptance

For students applying through the autumn cycle, notifications of host university placement are typically announced in mid-December.

After receiving your acceptance, you will need to complete a number of important procedures.

Search for Scholarships

There are many scholarship options available, so be sure to find one that suits your needs.

Apply to the Host University

UTokyo only nominates you to the partner university. You must complete the actual application yourself, including specifying your academic interests and study plans.

Arrange Accommodation

Some universities provide housing support. However, in some countries or regions, student housing may be limited, and you may need to find accommodation on your own.

Apply for a Visa and Insurance

These processes can take time, so it is important to apply well in advance.

In addition to the steps above, you will also need to book flights, register for courses, and complete other administrative procedures.

About Faculty Exchange Programs

While USTEP is open to students from all faculties, individual faculties such as the Faculty of Engineering and the Faculty of Economics also have their own agreements with overseas universities. Students and graduate students in these faculties can participate in exchange programs at their faculty's partner universities through their faculty office.

Since application procedures, deadlines, and available partner universities differ by faculty, you will need to contact the international exchange office of your faculty directly.

A recommendation letter from a faculty member in your department is also required when applying. We recommend consulting with a professor from your laboratory, seminar, or small class well in advance.
